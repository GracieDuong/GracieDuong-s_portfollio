PRODUCT THINKING · UX · BUSINESS ANALYSIS

TravelHelper — AI Chatbot for Travel Decision Support

Designed an AI-assisted travel booking experience that reduces decision friction through conversational guidance, recommendation flows, and human fallback support.

Role

Product / Business Analysis

Timeline

4 weeks

Tools

Figma, Miro

Focus

Conversational UX, BPMN, Service Design

Business Problem

Most travel booking platforms I’ve used have the same problem: too many filters, too many tabs, too many choices thrown at you at once.

That leads to:

  • People spending 20–30 minutes just comparing flights
  • Opening 5–6 tabs for hotels, visa, weather, transport
  • Getting stuck when something unexpected happens
  • Waiting hours for support to reply

The business impact isn’t subtle: booking abandonment, decision fatigue, and unnecessary support tickets.

I didn’t want to build another booking form. I wanted to build something that actually helps people decide.

User Pain Points

😵

Decision Fatigue

Users face too many travel options and struggle to compare information efficiently.

⏱

Time-Consuming Planning

Searching flights, hotels, visas, and weather separately creates friction.

❓

Support Frustration

Users become frustrated when systems cannot handle unclear requests or exceptions.

Problem Breakdown

Beyond the surface-level pain points, I broke down each user problem into: how it actually affects the experience and what product response I built to solve it.

User / Business Problem Impact on Experience Product / Service Response
Users feel overwhelmed by too many travel options Decision fatigue and slower booking decisions Guided conversational prompts
Unclear or incomplete inputs User frustration and drop-off risk Validation and re-prompt flow
Payment failures during checkout Booking abandonment Retry logic + alternative method suggestion
Lack of personalized recommendations Lower engagement and conversion Recommendation-based filtering
Users require support beyond chatbot capability Increased operational friction Human handover workflow

Product Thinking

Product Goal

  • simplify travel planning
  • reduce cognitive overload
  • guide users through booking decisions
  • provide support escalation when needed

Product Hypothesis

If users receive guided recommendations through conversational interaction, they will make travel decisions faster and with greater confidence — without feeling like they’re “working” to book a trip.

I didn’t just want a chatbot. I wanted a decision-support system disguised as a conversation.
