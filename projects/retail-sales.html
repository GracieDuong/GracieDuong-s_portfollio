O&F Retail Case Study

Sales Performance, Customer Segmentation & Operational Analysis

An end-to-end analytics case study exploring how discount strategy, customer segmentation, and fulfillment operations impacted profitability and long-term customer value.

View Dashboard GitHub Repository Back to Portfolio

Business Context

O&F is a retail company operating across North Asia, Oceania, and Southeast Asia.

Although the business showed strong revenue growth and expanding market activity, deeper analysis revealed operational inefficiencies affecting profitability, customer experience, and retention.

This project approached the business as a connected system:

Customer → Purchase → Discount → Delivery → Profit → Retention

Key Findings

Finding 01

Revenue Growth Was Eroding Profitability

  • Products discounted above 40% frequently generated negative profit
  • Some transactions recorded losses up to -$2.7K
  • Revenue growth relied heavily on discount-driven sales

Business Impact: The business was optimizing for revenue growth rather than sustainable profitability.

Finding 02

Customer Segmentation Was Underutilized

  • “Others” generated the highest revenue contribution at around $1.12M
  • High-value “Champion” customers were underutilized
  • Customer segments were not integrated into business decisions

Business Impact: High-value customers were treated similarly to lower-value segments, limiting retention and upsell opportunities.

Finding 03

Delivery Performance Was Weakening Customer Experience

  • Late order rates reached 35–45%
  • Southeast Asia experienced the highest delays
  • Standard shipping consistently underperformed

Business Impact: Operational efficiency was prioritized over customer experience, increasing retention risk for valuable customers.

Recommendations

Pricing

Introduce discount thresholds and track profit-per-order.

Customer Strategy

Prioritize loyalty and upsell campaigns for high-value segments.

Operations

Introduce priority shipping and optimize Southeast Asia logistics.

Retention

Develop lifecycle-based engagement and re-engagement campaigns.

Final Insight

The core issue was not weak sales performance.

The business was growing, but the system behind that growth was not optimized for long-term profitability or customer value.

This project focused on identifying operational trade-offs, root causes, and strategic opportunities through a product and business analysis mindset.
